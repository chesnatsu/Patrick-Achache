Extending a Hand: Relief Mission to Earthquake-Affected Communities in Cebu

On the heels of the recent devastating earthquake that struck Cebu and surrounding areas, I had the opportunity to be part of a heartfelt mission — delivering much-needed relief goods to communities whose lives were suddenly shaken, both literally and figuratively. It was a humbling experience that reminded me of the power of compassion, solidarity, and community.

Reaching Out Where It Matters Most

Disasters hit the vulnerable hardest. We quickly mobilized resources and coordinated with local partners. Our goal was to get relief to those in greatest need, efficiently and swiftly. Our team visited several hard-hit municipalities, bringing essential supplies such as food, clean water, hygiene kits, and basic necessities to support the local communities. Here’s a quick overview of the areas we reached:

  • Borbon, Cebu – We gave relief goods to 150 families deeply affected by the quake. Many had damaged homes and struggled to access essentials.
  • Daanbantayan, Cebu – We distributed aid to 20 families, and the deep gratitude shown by the recipients was a moving testament to their resilience.
  • Compostela, Cebu – We distributed relief to 10 families. Each had their own story of resilience and recovery.

Each location presented its own challenges, but also its own moments of inspiration. From the tireless local volunteers to the smiles of children despite the harsh conditions, the spirit of these communities was unbreakable.

More Than Just Relief

Delivering goods was just one part of our mission. We aimed to demonstrate the presence and solidarity these communities deserve, making sure they knew we stood with them during this difficult time. We believe that small acts of kindness can bring healing. The road to recovery is long, but we are committed to standing with these communities and offering ongoing support.

Supporting the Youngest Victims of STS Trami: Patrick Achache’s Aid to Dr. Felipe Cevallos Elementary School

On October 22, Severe Typhoon, locally known as Kristine, struck the Philippines with devastating force, triggering heavy rainfall, widespread flooding, and landslides. Among the hardest-hit were vulnerable communities, including children, who were left with little to no resources as they braced for recovery.

Achache’s efforts are part of his ongoing commitment to disaster relief and education support, recognizing that the needs of students and their families go beyond immediate recovery. The Iffze300 Initiative: Cycling for a Cause

The Iffze300 initiative is a fundraising event involving a challenging 700 km cycling journey from Iffezheim to Hoppegarten. The event, inspired by the longstanding partnership between the two municipalities due to their shared interest in horse racing, aims to raise funds for various charitable causes.

The primary beneficiaries of the funds raised by Iffze300 are:

Patrick’s Altruism spread to DMD society

DMD also known Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy, this condition is due to the mutations of the dystrophin gene which is the most common Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy and severe type of muscular dystrophy.

DMD is the most common childhood onset form of muscular dystrophy and affects males almost exclusively. The birth prevalence is estimated to be 1 in every 3,500 live male births. Age of onset is usually between 3 and 5 years of age.

It is characterized by muscle weakness and muscular atrophy of the pelvis and thighs followed by the shoulder and arm muscles. It usually begins around the age of four and worsens quickly. This can result in trouble standing up. Most are unable to walk by the age of 12. Affected muscles may look larger due to increased fat content. Scoliosis is also common. Some may have intellectual disability.

As one of Patrick’s friends ran a charity for the DMD society, Patrick unreluctantly supported the cause as he can never turn his back to those who are in need. With the collective efforts of Patrick and his friend, the profits of the very successful campaign and had helped the Duchenne society.

Service to others is the rent you pay for your room here on earth

“Service to others is the rent you pay for your room here on earth.” Cebu Super Typhoon Odette Action Super Typhoon Rai, locally known as Odette, made its first landfall, bringing torrential rains, violent winds, floods and storm surges to the Visayas and Mindanao Islands. Overnight, the Typhoon left hundreds dead and hundreds of thousands of families homeless.

Initial reports showed devastation across many areas of the impacted regions, but it is only over the subsequent weeks that the full scale of damage has come to light. The initial assessments only hinted at the scale of destruction – nearly 1.7 million houses damaged or destroyed, massive damage to infrastructure, agricultural land, fishing communities and livelihoods across a vast geographic area of the country. In total, the Typhoon severely affected an estimated 9.9 million people across the six worst hit regions, leaving about 2.4 million people in need of assistance. More than a month on, nearly 144,000 people remain displaced, and many more are living in damaged shelters with little access to basic services.

Quickly after Cebu was short of everything. No drinking water, no electricity, no signal, no internet, no transportation, no gasoline, no food. The government declares state of calamity as Typhoon Odette leaves Patrick despite being thousands of kilometers away from the disaster did everything possible to lessen the burden and pain and helped where he could. He provided financial assistance for over 70+ families to rebuild their totally damaged homes, provided over 40 families with a drinking water filter systems to generate drinking water without any chemical or electricity use since drinking water was hard to find and locals got sick from drinking dirty water from the nearby rivers, sends canned goods to affected provinces, solar lamps, generators and so on.

Standing with Het Sikkelcelfonds: Why I Support the Fight Against Sickle Cell Disease

https://hetsikkelcelfonds.nl/

Sometimes a cause resonates so deeply, you must speak out. For me, that cause is sickle cell disease. I am proud to support Het Sikkelcelfonds, a Dutch foundation dedicated to addressing this genetic condition.

What Is Sickle Cell Disease?

Cell Disease (SCD) is a hereditary disorder that causes red blood cells to become rigid and sickle-shaped. These cells can block blood vessels, reduce oxygen flow, and break down prematurely, resulting in pain, organ damage, chronic fatigue, and sometimes early death.

Despite its significant impact, SCD receives limited attention in public health, especially in Europe. Each year, approximately 300,000 children are born with the disease worldwide, and many do not survive past childhood due to inadequate care.

SCD is both a health and social justice issue that requires greater attention.

Why I Support Het Sikkelcelfonds

Founded in 2017, Het Sikkelcelfonds is the only Dutch organization dedicated to sickle cell disease. The foundation raises awareness, supports patients, and funds research.

Their goals are clear and urgent.

  • Raise public awareness of SCD and its impact.
  • Fund innovative research toward better treatments and a cure.
  • Support patients and their families through advocacy and education.

Supporting Het Sikkelcelfonds advances health equity and compassion. No child should suffer from a preventable disease.

How You Can Help

You do not need to be a doctor or scientist to make a difference. Here are ways you can contribute.

  • Donate: Every contribution helps fund life-saving research. Donate here.
  • Share: Start conversations about sickle cell disease to raise awareness and inspire others to act.
  • Fundraise: Organize an event or campaign to help drive progress.
  • Learn: Visit hetsikkelcelfonds.nl to access vital information.

Supporting Het Sikkelcelfonds advances health equity and compassion. No child should suffer from a preventable disease.

Final Thoughts

Supporting Het Sikkelcelfonds is personal for me, not because I have the disease, but because I believe healthcare should be inclusive, research representative, and every life valued.

If this message resonates with you, take action now: share it widely, donate today, or become actively involved. Your support can make a real difference in helping eliminate sickle cell disease.

Let us raise our voices and take meaningful steps for those who need them most.
